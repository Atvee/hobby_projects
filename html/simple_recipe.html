Recipe for a kerala style poratta and beef

Kerala-Style Parotta and Beef Fry is a beloved South Indian dish known for its rich, bold flavors and irresistible textures. The flaky, layered parotta is pan-fried to golden perfection, serving as the ideal base to soak up the spicy, slow-roasted beef fry. The beef is typically marinated with a blend of earthy spices, cooked until tender, and then stir-fried with curry leaves, coconut slivers, and caramelized onions, creating a deeply aromatic and savory profile that defines Kerala’s culinary tradition.

Ingredients

  • Beef (boneless, cubed) – 500g
  • Shallots – 1 cup, sliced
  • Black pepper – 1 tsp, freshly ground
  • Curry leaves – 2 sprigs

Instructions

  1. Marinate beef with turmeric, chili powder, coriander, and salt; pressure cook until tender.
  2. In coconut oil, sauté shallots until browned; add curry leaves and crushed garlic.
  3. Add the cooked beef and roast on medium-high, stirring continuously until dry and well-coated.
  4. Finish with black pepper and optional coconut slices; serve hot with parotta.
